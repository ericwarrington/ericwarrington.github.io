Beyond the Tales

Project Dates: March - October 2015

Team Size: 5

Language: C# (using Unity engine)

Summary

Inspired by the early Final Fantasy and Zelda games, Beyond the Tales is a 2D JRPG centered around a young man named Joreld. Raised and trained in the way of the Knight by his father, a retired Knight Commander of the kingdom of Cairnmount, Joreld comes home one day and finds that his world is going to change forever.

I worked on this project with a group of my friends while in school. We had two teams, Development and Story; I served on the Dev team. One of the devs also produced original scores for the game. We used the Unity game engine and wrote our own RPG engine--on top of it--from scratch.

Responsibilities

The Development team was responsible for:

  • Acquiring tilesets and other graphical assets (most were open-license)
  • Designing and building maps
  • The entire game engine: battles, NPCs, talking, scripts, maps, map-warps, etc.
  • Designing/creating enemies, items, etc.
  • Designing and building the UI
  • Handling save data
  • Anything else that required programming

The Story team was responsible for:

  • Writing/creating all the story elements
  • Designing story-NPCs (dialogue)
  • Any requisite world-building
  • Any other story-related tasks

Personal Responsibilities

As a Game Developer on Beyond the Tales' Development team, I did a good portion of the research involved for finding map tilesets and enemy graphics. I also designed and implemented the process we used for warping between maps--this was mostly based off my experience making ROM hacks for 3rd-gen Pokémon games. I did a little bit of work on map designs and creation--some map editing, too--but most of our maps were simple and didn't necessarily follow good level design conventions. Finally, I was responsible for the saving/loading features. This required creating a "save data" data-structure and binary-serializing/deserializing it as necessary. The save data included map warp data (what map the player was on) and event flags (what story events had already ocurred). I also updated the menu logic to call the correct save/load methods, handle cases when no saves existed, and handle playing new games even when a previous save existed.
